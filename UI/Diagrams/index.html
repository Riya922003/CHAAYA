CHAAYA is an advanced early warning system that leverages real-time particle data from India's Aditya-L1 mission to detect and classify Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs) that pose threats to Earth's space environment. Our dual-stage machine learning approach provides crucial lead time for implementing protective measures for space assets and terrestrial infrastructure.

This system is designed to support ISRO's space weather monitoring capabilities and contribute to India's growing expertise in space-based early warning systems for protecting critical space infrastructure.

üîç How CHAAYA Works - Step by Step

Step 1 - Data Collection: The Aditya-L1 satellite continuously sends data about solar wind and particles to Earth. This data comes in real-time, 24/7.

Step 2 - Data Cleaning: The raw data is cleaned and organized. We remove any errors or missing information to ensure accuracy.

Step 3 - First Detection Stage: Our smart computer system looks for unusual patterns in the data that might indicate a CME (solar storm) is coming toward Earth.

Step 4 - Verification: When something unusual is found, the system double-checks to make sure it's really a CME and not just random noise.

Step 5 - Classification Stage: Once confirmed, the system determines how dangerous this CME might be - whether it's weak, moderate, or strong.

Step 6 - Alert Generation: Based on the danger level, CHAAYA sends appropriate alerts to ISRO scientists and satellite operators so they can take protective action.

Key Benefit: This entire process happens automatically and gives us 30-60 minutes of advance warning before a solar storm hits Earth's satellites.
